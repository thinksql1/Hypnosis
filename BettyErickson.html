Betty Erickson 3-2-1 Self-Hypnosis

A natural process to bridge into deeper altered states for sleep, inner work, lucid dreaming, astral projection, and more.

1. Preparation

  • Get comfortable: Find a safe, quiet place where you can sit or lie down without being disturbed.
  • Set your intention: Decide what you want to achieve. Examples include deep rest, inner work, lucid dreaming, astral travel, or creative problem-solving.
  • Choose a focal point: Gently rest your gaze on a spot on the wall, ceiling, or an object in front of you.
  • Slow your breathing: Inhale deeply through your nose, and exhale slowly through your mouth. Allow your body to begin relaxing with each breath.

2. 3–2–1 Sensory Countdown (Eyes Open)

You will cycle through your senses of sight, hearing, and feeling, reducing the number of observations in each round.

The Sequence

3 Sights
3 Sounds
3 Feelings
→
2 Sights
2 Sounds
2 Feelings
→
1 Sight
1 Sound
1 Feeling

See (3x): "I see the pattern on the wall... I see the reflection in the window... I see the curve of the chair."

Hear (3x): "I hear the hum of the fan... I hear my breathing... I hear a distant sound outside."

Feel (3x): "I feel my body in the chair... I feel the air on my skin... I feel the weight of my feet on the floor."

Continue this pattern, describing two of each, and then one of each. There is no need to rush.

3. Imaginative Sensory Expansion (Eyes Closed)

Close your eyes. The process now moves from your external environment to your internal, imagined world.

1. Choose Your Place

Visualize a place you'd like to be—real, remembered, or entirely imagined. It could be a peaceful forest, a beach at sunset, a mountaintop, or a room you love.

2. Engage Your Senses Internally

Use the same pattern, but this time you are building the scene. Start with one of each, then add more.

Round 1 (1 of each):

"I see the sunlight on the water."

"I hear the gentle waves."

"I feel the warm sand under my feet."

Continue with a round of two sights, sounds, and feelings. Then a round of three, and so on. Allow yourself to become fully absorbed in the place you are creating.

4. Deepening

With each cycle of imagined senses, let the scene become more vivid and real. Allow your body to feel heavier and more relaxed, while your mind feels lighter and more expansive.

  • Imagine walking further into your scene.
  • Picture yourself descending a beautiful staircase.
  • Feel yourself gently floating deeper into comfort and peace.

5. Enter Your Chosen State

You are now in a deeply receptive state, fully in control. Direct your awareness toward your intention.

For Sleep

Allow the scene to dissolve naturally as you drift into a peaceful sleep.

For Inner Work

Bring your focus to insights, healing, or self-exploration within the safety of this state.

For Lucid Dreaming / Astral Projection

Imagine fully stepping into the dream or lifting gently away from your body.

For Creativity

Explore and interact with your imagined space, remaining open to inspiration.
